Katie & Grady · Shelterwood, Healdsburg

SAY THIS

On every call

"Hi — I'm booking wedding guest shuttles for [DATE], about [#] guests.

Route: a loop between three downtown Healdsburg hotels — Hotel Trio, Dry Creek Inn, and Hotel Vinea / Travelodge — and our venue, Shelterwood, about 20 minutes out.

Schedule: every 30 minutes, 3:30–5:00 pm, then again 9:00 pm–1:00 am. Split shift, ends at 1 am.

One thing to know: the access road is narrow, unpaved, steep, and unlit. What size vehicle do you recommend for that?"

Then ask all five

  1. Can you run until 1 am? Any late or overtime fee?
  2. What's your TCP number? (California license — no number, no booking.)
  3. Minimum hours? How does the split shift bill — do we pay for the 4-hour gap?
  4. Deposit and cancellation policy?
  5. Can you send a Certificate of Insurance naming the venue?

FYI

Read once, then forget

$What this should cost➤

Shuttle vans and mini-coaches run $100–300/hr per vehicle, with 3–5 hour minimums. A whole shuttle program typically lands $600–2,500; multi-vehicle setups $1,500–4,000+. Limo is $75–150/hr.

Carts are quote-based locally — the national benchmark is roughly $375–500 per cart plus ~$250 delivery. 4x4 UTVs around $200/day.

Add 18–20% gratuity, fuel surcharges, and possible overtime for the 1 am end. Get itemized quotes — the sticker rate is never the number.

!Book now, not later➤

Sonoma peak season is April through October and Saturdays sell out — Sprinters especially, since they're what everyone wants for hill venues. Standard advice is 3–6 months ahead.

Last-minute is possible with the big operators, but it costs more and you take whatever vehicle is left.

⚖Why we can't just use a guy with a van➤

California requires a CPUC TCP permit for anyone driving passengers for money. Licensed carriers carry $750K–$5M in liability (the $5M tier kicks in at 16+ passenger vehicles).

An unlicensed operator is driving illegally, almost certainly has no commercial insurance, and if something happens on that road in the dark, we're the ones exposed. Fun as it sounds — licensed carriers only for passengers.

Save the informal help for hauling décor. That part we can absolutely crowdsource.

⚲Confirm the address before anyone drives➤

Two properties near Healdsburg use the Shelterwood name — the old Mill Creek glamping camp (lost in the 2020 Walbridge Fire) and Shelterwood Collective, a 900-acre forest above the Russian River.

Send every driver the exact GPS pin, gate code, and a venue contact. Cell service is thin up there — if they get lost at 12:40 am, nobody's reaching anybody.

⚠Brokers vs. the actual company➤

CharterUP, National Charter Bus, Price4Limo and similar are marketplaces — they subcontract to whoever's free. Quotes come back fast, which is tempting.

But confirm who's actually showing up, their TCP, and whether that specific vehicle fits the road, before any deposit changes hands.
